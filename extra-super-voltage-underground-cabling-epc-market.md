PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) cables are cost-effective and commonly used for various applications but have limitations in temperature and flexibility. XLPE (Cross-Linked Polyethylene) cables offer superior thermal resistance and durability, making them suitable for high-voltage applications. Rubber cables are flexible, weather-resistant, and ideal for dynamic environments, providing excellent insulation but can be more expensive. Each type serves specific needs in underground electrical infrastructure.</p></p>
